Introduction

Gender inequality is a prevalent issue that has been seen throughout Indian society, and is often exemplified in Bollywood as well. As early as the 1950s, women in Bollywood films were portrayed from the male perspective, often made out to be symbols of either good or evil that the male protagonist encounters on their journey. Over the next decade, this characterization remained largely the same, with the “angry young man” and the demure, male-dependant female lead who only served to illustrate the “ideal Indian woman”. The 1980s witnessed the widespread popularity of melodramas, where Bollywood films attempted to reinvent the female persona by using the “upper-middle class” status while still holding on to their traditional roots. It is only in the 2000s that there started to be a significant shift in how women were portrayed in films. The female protagonists were now well educated and capable of supporting their families, and were no longer restricted to solely looking after the house or taking care of the male leads. Finally, the 2010s led to a boom in female-centric films that had little to no male focus. Women were allowed to be independent, less traditional, and fully capable of making their own life decisions without the need for male input. Through our research, we will be encompassing the changes Bollywood has gone through with regards to gender inequality from the 1950s to the present.
